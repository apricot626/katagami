How to make a baby hat (tulip hat)

A round crown of six petals (gores) with a downward brim. Make it with an outer and a lining and it's fully reversible. Cute sun protection for baby outings.

1. Materials & tools

Materials (baby hat)

  • Outer fabric (double gauze, light cotton) — about 50 cm square
  • Lining (same; a contrast for reversible) — about 50 cm square
  • Optional: twill tape or elastic for a chin strap, a decorative button

Tools

  • Sewing machine (or needle and thread)
  • Fabric marker or pencil
  • Ruler and scissors
  • Iron
  • Pins or clips

3. Cutting the fabric

  1. Cut the petals
    Cut 6 petals. For reversible, cut 6 outer + 6 lining (12 total).
  2. Cut the brim
    Fold the fabric in quarters and place the brim's fold edges on the folds so it opens into a ring; cut one outer and one lining.
  3. Prepare curves
    Plan to clip the curved seam allowances so they turn smoothly.

4. Sewing

4-1. Make the crown

  1. Sew the petals right sides together along their curved edges, one after another, then close into a ring to form a dome.
  2. Clip the curves and press the allowances open at the top.
  3. Make an outer crown and a lining crown.

4-2. Make the brim

  1. Sew the outer and lining brims right sides together around the outer (large) circle.
  2. Clip the seam, turn right side out and topstitch around the outer edge.

4-3. Join the crown and brim

  1. Sew the outer crown's opening to the inner edge of the brim, right sides together.
  2. Tuck the lining crown's allowance in and slip-stitch or topstitch it to finish (reversible). Press.

5. Tips & variations

  • Reversible: two fabrics give two hats in one.
  • Chin strap: add a tie or elastic so it stays on in the wind.
  • Sun protection: a deeper brim shades little faces.
