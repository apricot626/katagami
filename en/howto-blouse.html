How to make a blouse

A button-down blouse with set-in sleeves: the back is cut on the fold and the front as two pieces with a button stand. Best in woven fabric. Casual or smart depending on your fabric.

1. Materials & tools

Materials (adult blouse)

  • Woven fabric (shirting, double gauze, linen) — 110 cm wide × about 200 cm
  • Buttons (1–1.2 cm) — 7–9
  • Interfacing for the button stand and neckline — a little
  • Bias tape for the neckline — the neckline length

Tools

  • Sewing machine (or needle and thread)
  • Fabric marker or pencil
  • Ruler and scissors
  • Iron
  • Pins or clips

2. Make the pattern (auto-generated in Katagami)

With the Katagami pattern tool, you just enter the finished size and it drafts a seam-allowance-included pattern, tiled to print at actual size on Letter or A4.

  1. Open the tool
    Open the pattern tool and choose “Adult clothes” → “Blouse” from the tabs at the top.
  2. Enter the size
    Enter bust, shoulder, length, sleeve and the button-stand width. Presets cover S–XL.
  3. Set the seam allowance
    Use the slider at the bottom (beginners: 1.0–1.5 cm / about 3/8–5/8 in).
  4. Print
    Press “Print (actual size)”, then in the print dialog set Scale = 100% and turn “Fit to page” OFF. Check the 50 mm calibration box on the first guide sheet with a ruler.

3. Cutting the fabric

  1. Cut the back
    Place the center-back on the fold and cut 1.
  2. Cut the fronts
    Cut 1 with the front pattern, then flip it and cut a mirror image (2 total).
  3. Cut the sleeves
    Cut 2 sleeves.
  4. Finish the edges
    Finish the seam-allowance edges; the front button-stand edge depends on your finish.

4. Sewing

4-1. Sew the shoulders and sides

  1. Sew the front and back at the shoulders and sides, right sides together; press open.

4-2. Finish the neckline

  1. Measure the neckline and cut bias tape to fit.
  2. Sew the bias tape around the neckline, turn it in and press.

4-3. Set in the sleeves

  1. Match the sleeve-cap notch to the shoulder seam and sew each sleeve into the armhole; press toward the sleeve.

4-4. Sew the underarm/side in one line

  1. Sew from the sleeve cuff down to the hem in one continuous seam; press.

4-5. Finish the front opening

  1. Fold back the button stand on each front, press and topstitch.
  2. Mark and make the buttonholes, then sew the buttons on the opposite side.

4-6. Hem

  1. Double-fold and press the hem, then topstitch 2 mm from the edge.

5. Tips & variations

  • Crisp collar area: interface the button stand and neckline.
  • Sleeve length: shorten for short sleeves or add a cuff.
  • Fabric: double gauze is soft; shirting is crisp and smart.
